Wee Quests: UX Case Study

About the Project

What is Wee Quests?

Wee Quests aims to let people discover Scotland through app-led scavenger hunts.

The app will list hunts that have been pre-designed by locals and can be purchased by users. Players will also be able to create their own scavenger hunts to share with friends.

What was my Role?

Wee Quests is a solo project, designed for my UX Immersion Course with CareerFoundry. My goals were to:

  • Implement design thinking and gain solid, hands-on experience across the UX design process
  • Create an interactive prototype for a scavenger hunt web app in response to my selected course brief

My Challenge

To be effective as a scavenger hunt app, Wee Quests would need to motivate people to make the move from simply viewing the app, to actually going outdoors and taking a scavenger hunt.

How did the Design evolve?

Wee Quests’ UI has been shaped by elements from across my UX design process.

Competitor research and user interviews laid the foundations for what I wanted to achieve. I then built up my design through an iterative process of prototyping and user testing. Finally, design psychology and peer review helped to refine the interface.

My Design Process

Researching the Project

Competitor Research

I began my research by carrying out an analysis of competitor apps. I discovered a gap in the market for a Scotland-focussed scavenger hunt app aimed solely at individuals rather than also at businesses running team-building activities.

Surveys and Interviews

With the help of a survey, I defined my target audience as 25-40 year olds who enjoy travel and activities that take them off the beaten track. I then conducted in-person interviews to find out more about their motivations and frustrations.

Persona and User Flows

Using data gathered in my research, I went on to form persona and user flows representative of my target audience. This allowed me to clearly define my users’ goals and provided a reference for prototyping that would ensure my design stayed on track to meet their specific needs.

Prototyping and Testing

Wireframing and Prototyping

My starting point for prototyping was that my target audience would be motivated by an app that was fun, friendly and easy to use.

My aim was to suit both Marco, who wanted to quickly plan an interesting activity as a tourist, and Nina, who wanted to take her time to create a custom experience to share with friends in her local area.

Usability Testing

Once I had a black and white interactive prototype ready, I ran five moderated usability tests to check for issues and gauge user satisfaction.

Refining the Design

Visual Design

Once I had rectified issues identified in testing, I moved my focus to refining the visual and emotional design of the app. As I worked on converting Wee Quests into colour, I drew on Gestalt principles, colour psychology, Material Design and web accessibility guidelines.

My goals were to encourage engagement and interaction with the app and to enhance ease of use by reducing cognitive load.

A Closer Look:

1. Building on positive feedback in testing, I further developed the app’s personality. The way the mascot’s poses interact with UI elements is designed to add engagement and a sense of fun

2. I updated the language on call outs to be more conversational and to allow users to make proactive choices

3. Meanwhile, I updated UI elements where necessary to ensure a strong and consistent hierarchy that would help guide users through tasks. Here, I want the user’s first instinct on the second screen to be to add another stop and not to preview the hunt

4. Following peer review, I moved the introduction of the tips feature to the beginning of the Create a Hunt feature. Therefore users are onboarded early and tips are optional by the time users are further into the process

5. The overall aim of the tips icon is to strike the balance between my audience’s need for tutorials and the need to give users freedom as they create their own hunt

Wee Quests Prototype

Arriving at the Design

The finished prototype aims to offer a fun, friendly and easy to use web app experience that encourages people to head outdoors and explore their home area and Scotland beyond.
